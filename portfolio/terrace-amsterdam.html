Our Story

A canal house kitchen

Terrace was born in 2018 when chef Joris van der Berg took over a 17th-century canal house on the Herengracht and turned its ground floor into an open kitchen. The building's original oak beams and hand-laid tiles remain, and the terrace stretches out over the water in summer, catching the last of the golden-hour light that Amsterdam does so well. Joris trained in Lyon and Copenhagen before returning home, bringing with him a conviction that the best cooking starts with restraint and impeccable ingredients.

Everything on our menu traces back to growers, fishermen, and producers we know by name. Dutch asparagus in spring, North Sea cod landed that morning, Beemster beef raised on open pastures. We do not import what the season cannot provide. The result is a menu that changes weekly, sometimes daily, and a kitchen that feels more like a conversation between the land and the plate. That simplicity is the point.
